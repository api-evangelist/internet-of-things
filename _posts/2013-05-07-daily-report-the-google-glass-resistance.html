The glasseslike device allows Internet access, as well as the shooting of photos and video, raising concerns about privacy and distraction.A Seattle bar has pre-emptively banned it.Las Vegas casinos are making it clear that wearers will not be welcome.Google stresses that Glass is a work in progress, with test versions being released to 2,000 developers.Safeguards are being put it place to make it less intrusive: for example, you have to speak or touch it to activate it, and you have to look directly at someone to shoot a photo or video of them.
