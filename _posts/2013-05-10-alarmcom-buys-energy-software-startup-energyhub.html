The move shows how energy efficiency services sold to utilities and energy providers are becoming an increasingly attractive business.The news follows the announcement earlier this week that smart thermostat company Nest acquired an energy data company called MyEnergy.Alarm.com is a Virginia-based 13-year-old company that makes wireless security and energy management systems that use cellular networks and mobile apps to enable customers to manage their homes.The security systems have connected video cameras, and door, window and cabinet sensors, while the home management system has connected thermostats and controllable lighting.
