To kick-start the Internet of Things era, International Business Machines Corporation (NYSE:IBM) has partnered up with ARM Holdings (NASDAQ:ARMH) to supply developers with Internet of Things (IoT) starter kits to push development of connected devices and start integrating them within households.ARM, a British semiconductor company, will provide the development kits, whose specification includes the ARM Cortex – M4 processing core which is going to clock in at 120MHz—,an Ethernet connection link on the board, as well as other components like a 128 x 32 LCD screen, 256 KB of RAM, and 1 MB of flash storage.The kit also includes a speaker, a joystick, temperature sensor, accelerometer, and potentiometers.This little kit packs quite a punch for the hobbyist who would like to connect his home to the Internet.Moreover, IBM is also providing its BlueMix cloud service which will help developers save, connect and execute their processes through the cloud.
